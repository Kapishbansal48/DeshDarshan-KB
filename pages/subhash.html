Subhash Chandra Bose

Indian Freedom Fighter | Netaji

Subhash Chandra Bose (1897–1945), popularly known as Netaji, was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement against British rule. He advocated armed struggle and formed the Indian National Army (INA) to fight for India’s freedom.

👶 Early Life

  • Born on 23 January 1897 in Cuttack, Odisha.
  • Full name: Subhash Chandra Bose.
  • Studied at Presidency College, Kolkata, and later joined the Indian Civil Services (ICS).
  • Resigned from ICS to join the Indian independence movement.

🕊️ Role in Freedom Struggle

  • Joined the Indian National Congress and rose as a prominent leader.
  • Advocated complete independence from British rule.
  • Formed the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army) in 1942 in Southeast Asia.
  • Collaborated with Japan to fight against the British in India.
  • Famous slogan: "Give me blood, and I will give you freedom!"

🏆 Achievements

  • Successfully mobilized Indian expatriates and soldiers to join the INA.
  • Boosted Indian nationalism and inspired freedom fighters across India.
  • Played a pivotal role in modernizing the Indian nationalist movement with armed struggle.
  • Remembered as one of India’s most courageous and strategic leaders.

🌟 Legacy

  • Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose is commemorated on India’s Independence Day and Republic Day.
  • Many institutions, streets, and airports named after him (e.g., Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata).
  • His life continues to inspire youth and freedom fighters in India.
