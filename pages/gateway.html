Gateway of India

Iconic Monument of Mumbai | Built in 1924

Welcome to the Gateway of India

A majestic symbol of Mumbai overlooking the Arabian Sea.

Key Facts

  • Location: Mumbai, Maharashtra, India
  • Constructed: 1924, designed by George Wittet
  • Height: 26 meters (approx.)
  • Style: Indo-Saracenic architecture
  • Material: Yellow Basalt and Reinforced Concrete

Historical Significance

  • Built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911.
  • Symbol of British colonial rule and later India’s independence.
  • The last British troops departed through this gateway in 1948.
  • Today, it is one of Mumbai’s most visited landmarks.

Architecture

  • Designed in the Indo-Saracenic style with hints of Hindu and Muslim motifs.
  • Massive central arch flanked by two smaller arches.
  • Constructed using yellow basalt and concrete.
  • Faces directly towards the Arabian Sea.
  • Serves as a starting point for ferry rides to Elephanta Caves.

Visitor Information

  • Timings: Open 24 hours
  • Entry: Free
  • Best Time: November – February (pleasant weather)
  • Nearby attractions: Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, Marine Drive, Elephanta Caves
